# Commercial Licensing and Commercial-Use Boundary

Status: public commercial-use boundary  
This document is not legal advice.

## Purpose

AAEF-AI-VA is publicly licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0.

This document explains the public commercial-use boundary and the existence of a
possible separate commercial licensing discussion. It does not grant a commercial
license by itself and does not define final commercial terms.

## Public license

AAEF-AI-VA code is licensed under AGPL-3.0.

Organizations may review, use, modify, and redistribute the project under the terms
of AGPL-3.0.

## Separate commercial licensing discussion

Organizations that want to embed, modify, integrate, operate, or provide services
using AAEF-AI-VA in a way that does not align with AGPL-3.0 obligations may request
a separate commercial licensing discussion with the project author.

Commercial licensing inquiries are separate from vulnerability reports.

Commercial inquiries do not authorize:

- runtime execution,
- live scanning,
- external network testing,
- credential injection,
- customer-target operation,
- production deployment,
- compliance certification,
- legal approval,
- audit opinion.

## No implied commercial grant

No separate commercial license is granted unless a separate written agreement is
executed by the project author or an authorized rights holder.

A GitHub issue, pull request, fork, star, release note, README statement, email
conversation, or public discussion is not a commercial license grant.

## Service-provider and MSSP use

Service-provider, MSSP, consulting, or managed-service use may raise additional
licensing, contractual, operational, and security questions.

Organizations considering such use should discuss licensing, scope, attribution,
support, liability, disclosure, and operational boundaries before deployment.

## Private proposal materials

Pricing assumptions, target-customer lists, proposal drafts, negotiation notes, and
customer-specific materials should remain outside the public repository, for example
under `private-not-in-git/`.

## Security reports

Security reports must follow SECURITY.md.

Do not use public issues for sensitive vulnerability details.

## No legal advice

This document is an operational and project-boundary statement. It is not legal
advice. Organizations should consult qualified counsel before relying on it for
licensing or commercial decisions.
